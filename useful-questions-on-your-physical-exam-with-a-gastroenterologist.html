Useful Questions On Your Physical Exam With A Gastroenterologist

So you have a scheduled visit to a gastroenterologist. You are now one step closer to a diagnosis and treatment, and having better GI health. During your first visit, the GI doctor will be performing a physical exam. This moment is perfect for addressing concerns or asking critical questions about your health.

In order to make the most out of your visit, it is best to be actively engaged. There is no doubt you have questions about your condition. Below, we will give you some useful questions you can ask your gastroenterologist.

What Tests Do I Need?

Before your GI doctor makes a diagnosis or recommends any treatment, they will need to do some tests. It is important to know which tests will be needed, how they are going to be carried out, how you can prepare for them, and how soon you can get the results. Some of the tests your GI doctor may recommend are either a breath test, blood test, stool test, endoscopy, or colonoscopy, depending on the nature of your symptoms and medical history.

Are there any Lifestyle Changes I Should Make?

Many gastrointestinal issues can be managed with simple lifestyle modifications, such as avoiding certain foods or increasing fiber intake. Ask your GI doctor about specific dietary changes that you can make that could help improve your digestion and overall gastrointestinal health, so you can take proactive steps toward managing your symptoms.

When Should I Come Back for Follow-up Visits?

Depending on the severity of the issue being treated, it is essential to stay up-to-date with regular checkups in order to monitor the condition and determine how you are responding to treatment. GI doctors can make adjustments, if necessary. Do not miss follow-up visits with your gastroenterologist, so you receive comprehensive care.

What Medications Might I Need?

In some cases, medication may be necessary in order for proper treatment and symptom management of gastrointestinal issues such as heartburn, acid reflux, stomach ulcers, GERD (gastroesophageal reflux disease), or irritable bowel syndrome (IBS). Be sure to ask about potential side effects associated with any prescribed medications before taking them.

Asking these questions will ensure that all bases are covered when visiting a gastroenterologist, and that you learn about your condition, how to properly manage it, and improve your digestive health. Should you have any questions not mentioned here, feel free to ask your GI doctor.
