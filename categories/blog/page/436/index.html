Why do designers use tonal shading on their sketches?

published on July 04, 2024
Answer What is the purpose of tonal shading in design? Tonal shading is used by designers to assist differentiate which sides of a drawing will be exposed to the sun the most, as well as to enhance the appearance of the artwork and “make” it seem more 3D. To put it another way, what makes tonal sketching so effective? While linear drawing encourages the establishment of boundaries, tonal drawing seeks to dissolve these boundaries while emphasising the quality of light and atmosphere that links all things in the viewer’s field of vision.
