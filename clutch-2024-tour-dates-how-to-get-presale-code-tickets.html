Clutch: The Road Warriors

Before we jump into the juicy tour details, let’s take a quick pit stop and rev up our engines with a little background on Clutch. Formed in 1991 in Maryland, this American rock band has been tearing up the stage for over three decades. The original lineup – Dan Maines, Jean-Paul Gaster, Tim Sult, and Roger Smalls – laid the foundation for what would become a legendary musical journey. Neil Fallon later stepped in for Smalls, adding his own flair to the mix.Despite facing some early challenges, including a label change, Clutch powered through with relentless touring. The breakthrough moment came in 1995 with their self-titled album. However, a label shakeup didn’t dampen their spirits, and they continued to evolve, giving us hits like “The Mob Goes Wild” and albums such as “Earth Rocker” and “Book of Bad Decisions.”Fast forward to 2022, and Clutch dropped their thirteenth album, “Sunrise on Slaughter Beach,” proving that they are still masters of their craft. The members also dabbled in side projects, solidifying their impact on the rock music scene.
