Woody Harrelson Biography: Age, Height, Net Worth, Movies, Wife, Wikipedia, Instagram

Woodrow Tracy Harrelson, professionally known as Woody Harrelson, is a distinguished American actor who has received numerous accolades throughout his career.

He gained recognition for his portrayal of bartender Woody Boyd on the popular NBC sitcom Cheers from 1985 to 1993, which earned him a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series. 

Harrelson further solidified his talent by reprising his role in the 1999 spinoff series Frasier, earning him another Primetime Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Guest Actor in a Comedy Series.

His impressive resume also includes nominations for three Academy Awards and four Golden Globe Awards.

Early Life

Woodrow Tracy Harrelson, known as Woody Harrelson, was born in Midland, Texas, to his mother Diane and father Charles Voyde Harrelson. 

His upbringing was in a Presbyterian household with his two brothers, Jordan and Brett. Unfortunately, their father, Charles, was convicted of the assassination of federal judge John H. Wood Jr. in 1979 and received a life sentence for the crime. 

Woody mentioned that his father was not present much during childhood. Charles passed away in 2007 while serving his sentence at the United States Penitentiary, Administrative Maximum Facility.

Education

Harrelson’s family faced financial struggles, depending on his mother’s income to make ends meet.

Despite their hardships, he attended Briarwood School in Houston, Texas. In 1973, Harrelson moved significantly to Lebanon, Ohio, his mother’s hometown.

There, he enrolled at Lebanon High School and successfully graduated in 1979. During the summer of that same year, he gained work experience at Kings Island amusement park.

While at Hanover College in Hanover, Indiana, Woody Harrelson pursued studies in theatre and English.

Alongside his academic pursuits, he also joined the Sigma Chi fraternity. He developed a friendship with fellow student Mike Pence, who would later become the Vice President of the United States.

Harrelson completed his studies and graduated from Hanover College in 1983, equipped with a strong foundation in theatre and English that would later pave the way for his successful acting career.

Career

Known for his versatility and talent, the actor rose to prominence through his role as Woody Boyd on the beloved sitcom Cheers. 

His success on television paved the way for a thriving career in film, where he showcased his comedic chops in movies like Doc Hollywood and White Men Can’t Jump.

However, his dramatic performances in films such as The People vs. Larry Flynt truly demonstrated his acting range, earning him critical acclaim and an Oscar nomination. 

He has become a respected and versatile actor in television and film with notable roles in films like Natural Born Killers, Indecent Proposal, and No Country for Old Men.

Woody Harrelson has built a remarkable and varied career in television and film. He has garnered critical acclaim for his performances in movies like The Messenger (2009), which earned him an Oscar nomination, and the fan-favorite Zombieland (2009).

He transitioned into a bankable action star with The Hunger Games franchise from 2012 to 2015 and showed his versatility as an actor. 

Harrelson continues to take on diverse projects, showcasing his talent in films like LBJ (2016) and Kate (2021).

Recently, audiences have seen him in The Highwaymen (2019), and he has exciting upcoming projects such as The Most Dangerous Man in America. In addition to his acting Work, he has ventured into producing successful shows like True Detective (2014-present). 

Beyond his on-screen endeavours, Woody Harrelson is also known for his environmental activism, using his platform to raise awareness on important issues.

Awards and nominations

  • Emmy Awards: For his iconic role in Cheers, he won a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actor in a Comedy Series. He’s also received nominations for his Work in True Detective and Frasier.
  • Academy Awards: Woody Harrelson has been nominated for three Oscars! He was nominated for Best Actor for “The People vs. Larry Flynt” and Best Supporting Actor for both “The Messenger” and “Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri.”
  • Golden Globe Awards: He’s garnered four Golden Globe nominations.
  • Screen Actors Guild Awards: Woody Harrelson has received seven Screen Actors Guild Awards nominations, including a win for Outstanding Performance by a Cast in a Motion Picture for “Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri.”

Personal Life

Throughout his life, Woody Harrelson has experienced a series of interesting moments. In 1985, he spontaneously married Nancy Simon, the daughter of playwright Neil Simon, in Tijuana.

Although they had initially planned to annul the marriage, they divorced in 1986. 2008, Harrelson married Laura Louie, his former assistant and co-founder of an organic food delivery service. The couple is still happily together and has three daughters: Deni, Zoe, and Makani. 

They currently reside in Maui, Hawaii, in a self-sustaining community. Harrelson is well-known for his vegan lifestyle and activism for various environmental and social causes.

Filmography

  • Harrelson became famous on the television sitcom Cheers (1985–1993).
  • He made his film debut in 1986 with Wildcats and went on to star in several successful comedies in the 1990s, including Doc Hollywood (1991), White Men Can’t Jump (1992), and Indecent Proposal (1993).
  • He also took on more dramatic roles, such as in Natural Born Killers (1994), a controversial satire about media violence.
  • Harrelson continued to impress critics and audiences with his Work in the 2000s and 2010s.
  • He received his first Academy Award nomination for Best Actor for portraying Larry Flynt in The People vs. Larry Flynt (1996).
  • He earned further acclaim for his supporting roles in The Thin Red Line (1998), No Country for Old Men (2007), and The Messenger (2009), for which he received his second Academy Award nomination, this time for Best Supporting Actor.
  • He also starred in several commercially successful films, such as Zombieland (2009), The Hunger Games (2012) (and its sequels), and Rampart (2011).

In recent years, Harrelson has continued to act in a variety of films, including LBJ (2016), a biopic of Lyndon B. Johnson, Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri (2017), for which he received his third Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor, and The Highwaymen (2019).
