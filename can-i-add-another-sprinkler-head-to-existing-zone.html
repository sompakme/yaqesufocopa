Is it true that each sprinkler zone has a separate valve?

An irrigation system is often split into many zones, each of which supplies water to sprinkler heads in a separate part of the lawn or garden. Each zone is controlled by a valve that receives signals from a centrally positioned controller, which in turn controls the whole system. A common arrangement is for the valves to be housed inside one or more in-ground valve boxes.
